# Stay Hungry, Stay Foolish · 乔布斯斯坦福毕业演讲精华

> Demo 范文 B · 演讲/个人叙事型 · 用于测试 Sagmeister / Bass / Carson / Brody / Scher 风格 demo deck
> 公共领域，2005 年 Stanford Commencement Address · 中英对照
> 三段叙事：Connecting Dots / Love and Loss / Death

---

## 开场 · Opening

Thank you. I'm honored to be with you today at your commencement from one of the finest universities in the world. Truth be told, I never graduated from college, and this is the closest I've ever gotten to a college graduation.

谢谢。我今天很荣幸能和你们一起，参加这所世界顶尖大学之一的毕业典礼。说实话，我从未从大学毕业过，这是我离大学毕业最近的一次。

Today I want to tell you three stories from my life. That's it. No big deal. Just three stories.

今天，我想给你们讲我人生中的三个故事。就这些，不是什么大事，就是三个故事。

---

## 第一个故事 · Connecting the Dots

The first story is about connecting the dots. I dropped out of Reed College after the first six months but then stayed around as a drop-in for another 18 months or so before I really quit.

第一个故事，是关于把生命中的点点滴滴连接起来。我从里德学院读了六个月就退学了，但又留校旁听了大约十八个月才真正离开。

My biological mother was a young, unwed graduate student, and she decided to put me up for adoption. She felt very strongly that I should be adopted by college graduates. Everything was set for me to be adopted at birth by a lawyer and his wife. Except that when I popped out, they decided at the last minute that they really wanted a girl.

我的生母是一个未婚的年轻研究生，她决定把我送给别人收养。她坚持要让上过大学的人来收养我。一切都为我安排好——一个律师和他的太太将在我出生时领养我。但当我降生时，他们最后一刻决定他们想要一个女孩。

So my parents, who were on a waiting list, got a call in the middle of the night asking: "We have an unexpected baby boy. Do you want him?" They said: "Of course."

所以正在等待名单上的我父母半夜接到一个电话："我们有一个意外出生的男婴，你们要他吗？"他们说："当然要。"

It wasn't all romantic. I had no dorm room, so I slept on the floor in friends' rooms. I returned Coke bottles for the 5¢ deposits to buy food with. And I would walk the 7 miles across town every Sunday night to get one good meal a week at the Hare Krishna temple.

但这并不全是浪漫。我没有宿舍，只能睡在朋友房间的地板上。我靠捡可乐瓶换 5 美分硬币买饭吃。每个周日晚上，我步行 7 英里穿过城市，到一个克里希纳神庙吃一顿好饭。

I loved it. And much of what I stumbled into by following my curiosity and intuition turned out to be priceless later on.

我爱那段日子。我凭着好奇心和直觉偶然撞上的许多东西，后来都变得无价。

**You can't connect the dots looking forward; you can only connect them looking backwards. So you have to trust that the dots will somehow connect in your future.**

**你不能预先把点点滴滴连接起来；只有在回头看时才能发现它们之间的联系。所以你必须相信，那些点点滴滴会在你未来的某一天串起来。**

---

## 第二个故事 · Love and Loss

My second story is about love and loss. I was lucky — I found what I loved to do early in life. Woz and I started Apple in my parents' garage when I was 20. We worked hard, and in 10 years Apple had grown from just the two of us in a garage into a $2 billion company with over 4,000 employees.

第二个故事是关于爱与失去。我很幸运——我很早就发现了自己热爱的事。20 岁那年，沃兹和我在我父母的车库里创办了苹果。我们努力工作，十年后，苹果从车库里的两个人发展成了一家市值 20 亿美元、有 4000 多员工的公司。

And then I got fired. How can you get fired from a company you started? Well, as Apple grew, we hired someone who I thought was very talented to run the company with me. And for the first year or so, things went well. But then our visions of the future began to diverge, and eventually we had a falling out.

然后我被炒了。一个人怎么会被自己创办的公司开除？事情是这样的：随着苹果壮大，我们雇了一个我认为很有才华的人来和我一起经营公司。最初一年左右还不错，但后来我们对未来的愿景出现了分歧，最终闹翻了。

I was a very public failure. I really didn't know what to do for a few months.

我是一个非常公开的失败者。有几个月，我真的不知道该做什么。

But something slowly began to dawn on me — I still loved what I did. So I decided to start over. The heaviness of being successful was replaced by the lightness of being a beginner again. It freed me to enter one of the most creative periods of my life.

但有些东西慢慢开始在我心里苏醒——我依然爱我做的事。所以我决定重新开始。成功的沉重被重新做新手的轻盈取代。这种自由让我进入了人生中最有创造力的时期之一。

**Sometimes life is going to hit you in the head with a brick. Don't lose faith. The only way to do great work is to love what you do.**

**有时候生活会用一块砖头砸你的脑袋。不要丧失信心。做出伟大事情的唯一方法，是热爱你所做的事。**

---

## 第三个故事 · Death

My third story is about death. When I was 17, I read a quote that went something like: "If you live each day as if it was your last, someday you'll most certainly be right."

第三个故事是关于死亡。我 17 岁那年读到一句话，大意是："如果你把每一天都当作生命的最后一天来过，那么有一天你一定会是对的。"

For the past 33 years, I have looked in the mirror every morning and asked myself: "If today were the last day of my life, would I want to do what I am about to do today?" And whenever the answer has been "No" for too many days in a row, I know I need to change something.

在过去的 33 年里，我每天早上都对着镜子问自己："如果今天是我生命的最后一天，我会想做今天准备做的事吗？"每当连续太多天的答案是"不"时，我知道我需要改变了。

About a year ago, I was diagnosed with cancer. The doctors told me this was almost certainly a type of cancer that is incurable, and that I should expect to live no longer than three to six months. My doctor advised me to go home and get my affairs in order, which is doctor's code for "prepare to die."

大约一年前，我被诊断出癌症。医生告诉我这几乎肯定是一种无法治愈的癌症，预计我能活不超过三到六个月。医生建议我回家把事情安排好，这是医生用语，意思是"准备死亡"。

I lived with that diagnosis all day. Later that evening I had a biopsy, and they discovered it was a very rare form of pancreatic cancer that is curable with surgery.

我整天都在那个诊断下生活。当晚我做了活检，发现是一种非常罕见的、可以通过手术治愈的胰腺癌。

**No one wants to die. Even people who want to go to heaven don't want to die to get there. And yet death is the destination we all share.**

**没有人想死。即使想去天堂的人，也不想为了去天堂而死。但死亡是我们所有人共同的归宿。**

**Your time is limited, so don't waste it living someone else's life. Don't be trapped by dogma. Don't let the noise of others' opinions drown out your own inner voice.**

**你的时间有限，所以不要为别人而活。不要被教条束缚。不要让他人的喧嚣淹没你内心的声音。**

---

## 结语 · Closing

When I was young, there was an amazing publication called The Whole Earth Catalog. It was created by a fellow named Stewart Brand. On the back cover of their final issue was a photograph of an early morning country road. Beneath it were the words: "Stay Hungry. Stay Foolish."

我年轻时，有一本非常棒的杂志叫《全球目录》，由一个叫斯图尔特·布兰德的人创办。它的最后一期封底是一张清晨乡村公路的照片，下面写着："求知若饥，虚心若愚。"

It was their farewell message as they signed off. And I have always wished that for myself. And now, as you graduate to begin anew, I wish that for you.

那是他们告别时留下的话。我一直把这句话当作自己的愿望。而现在，你们毕业即将开始新的人生，我也把这句话送给你们。

**Stay Hungry. Stay Foolish.**

**求知若饥，虚心若愚。**

Thank you all very much.

非常感谢大家。
