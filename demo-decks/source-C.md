## 序章 · The Cup of Humanity · 人间之器

Tea began as a medicine and grew into a beverage. In China, in the eighth century, it entered the realm of poetry as one of the polite amusements. The fifteenth century saw Japan ennoble it into a religion of aestheticism — Teaism.

茶最初作为药物出现，后演化为一种饮品。在中国，公元八世纪，它进入诗的领域，成为文雅的消遣之一。十五世纪，日本将其升华为一种美学的宗教——茶道。

Teaism is a cult founded on the adoration of the beautiful among the sordid facts of everyday existence. It inculcates purity and harmony, the mystery of mutual charity, the romanticism of the social order.

茶道是一种崇拜，建立在对日常生活中污浊事实之间的美的礼赞之上。它教人以纯净与和谐，教人理解相互慈悲的奥秘，教人体味社会秩序中的浪漫。

It is essentially a worship of the Imperfect, as it is a tender attempt to accomplish something possible in this impossible thing we know as life.

它在本质上是对「不完美」的崇拜，是在我们称之为人生的这个不可能之中，温柔地尝试完成某种可能。

The Philosophy of Tea is not mere aestheticism — it expresses conjointly with ethics and religion our whole point of view about man and nature. It is hygiene, for it enforces cleanliness; it is economics, for it shows comfort in simplicity rather than in the complex and costly; it is moral geometry, inasmuch as it defines our sense of proportion to the universe.

茶之哲学不只是美学——它结合伦理与宗教，表达了我们对人与自然的全部看法。它是卫生学，因它强调清洁；它是经济学，因它在简朴而非繁复昂贵中显出舒适；它是道德几何学，因它界定了我们与宇宙的比例感。

The outsider may indeed wonder at this seeming much ado about nothing. What a tempest in a tea-cup, he will say. But when we consider how small after all the cup of human enjoyment is, how soon overflowed with tears, how easily drained to the dregs in our quenchless thirst for infinity, we shall not blame ourselves for making so much of the tea-cup.

外人也许会惊讶于这看似的小题大做。他会说，这不过是茶杯里的风暴。但当我们想到，人生的享乐之杯毕竟多么微小，眼泪多么轻易就把它注满，我们在那永不止息的对无限的渴求中，又是多么轻易就把它一饮而尽——我们便不会责备自己为何如此看重这一只茶杯。

## 茶室 · The Tea-Room · 数寄屋

To European architects brought up on the traditions of stone and brick construction, our Japanese method of building with wood and bamboo seems scarcely worthy to be ranked as architecture. But what strikes one as most curious is that this seeming poverty of materials is intentional.

对于在石与砖的建筑传统中成长起来的欧洲建筑师而言，我们日本以木与竹为主的建造方式几乎不被视为建筑。但最令人惊奇的是，这种材料的「贫乏」，正是有意为之。

The simplicity and purism of the tea-room resulted from emulation of the Zen monastery. A Zen monastery differs from those of other Buddhist sects inasmuch as it is meant only to be a dwelling place for the monks. Its chapel is not a place of worship or pilgrimage, but a college room where the students congregate for discussion and the practice of meditation.

茶室的简素与纯粹，源于对禅院的效仿。禅院与其他佛教宗派的修道场不同，它仅作为僧人居所。其堂不为礼拜或朝圣之处，而是僧徒聚会讨论与修禅之所。

The tea-room (the Sukiya) does not pretend to be other than a mere cottage — a straw hut, as we call it. The original ideographs for Sukiya mean the Abode of Fancy. Latterly the various tea-masters substituted various Chinese characters according to their conception of the tea-room, and the term Sukiya may mean the Abode of Vacancy or the Abode of the Unsymmetrical.

茶室（数寄屋）不过是一间简陋的小屋——我们称之为「草庵」。「数寄屋」原本的字面意为「空想的居所」。后来，不同的茶匠依据他们对茶室的理解，替换了不同的汉字，「数寄屋」亦可意为「空虚的居所」或「不对称的居所」。

It is an Abode of Fancy inasmuch as it is an ephemeral structure built to house a poetic impulse. It is an Abode of Vacancy inasmuch as it is devoid of ornamentation except for what may be placed in it to satisfy some aesthetic need of the moment. It is an Abode of the Unsymmetrical inasmuch as it is consecrated to the worship of the Imperfect, purposely leaving some thing unfinished for the play of the imagination to complete.

它是「空想的居所」，因它是为安放一缕诗意而建的短暂结构。它是「空虚的居所」，因室内无任何装饰，除非为满足某一时刻的美感需要而临时陈设。它是「不对称的居所」，因它被献给对「不完美」的崇拜——故意留下某种未完成，让想象的游戏将其补全。

The simplicity of the tea-room and its freedom from vulgarity make it truly a sanctuary from the vexations of the outer world.

茶室之简素，及其对庸俗的远离，使它真正成为一处从外世烦扰中退避的圣所。

## 茶之流派 · Schools of Tea · 三派之分

There are three distinct schools of Tea: the Boiled Tea, the Whipped Tea, and the Steeped Tea. We moderns belong to the last school. These several methods of appreciating the beverage are indicative of the spirit of the age in which they prevailed.

茶有三个不同的流派：煎茶、抹茶、与煎泡之茶。我们这一代属于最后一派。这几种品茶的方式，分别表征了它们所流行时代的精神。

The Tang dynasty cake-tea which was boiled, the Sung dynasty powdered tea which was whipped, and the Ming dynasty leaf tea which was steeped, mark the distinct emotional impulses of the periods in which they were prevalent.

唐代的饼茶要煮，宋代的末茶要打，明代的散茶要泡——它们各自标志着所流行时代的不同情感冲动。

The Tang was classic, the Sung was romantic, and the Ming was the realist. Cha-king, the Holy Scripture of Tea, was written by Lu-wu in the middle of the eighth century. Cha-king consists of three volumes and ten chapters. He treats of the nature of the tea-plant, of the implements for gathering the leaves, of the selection of the leaves, of the brewing.

唐为古典，宋为浪漫，明为写实。茶之圣典《茶经》，由陆羽于八世纪中叶所撰，凡三卷十章。书中论及茶树之性、采叶之器、择叶之法、烹煮之道。

In the Sung dynasty, the whipped tea came into fashion and created the second school of Tea. The leaves were ground to fine powder in a small stone mill, and the preparation was whipped in hot water by a delicate whisk made of split bamboo.

至宋代，抹茶兴起，形成茶之第二派。茶叶在小石磨中研为细末，置于热水之中，以剖竹精制的茶筅击拂之。

It was at this time, however, that real tea began for us. In the latter half of the fifteenth century, under the patronage of the Shogun Ashikaga Yoshimasa, the tea-ceremony was fully constituted and made into an independent and secular performance.

然而正是在此时，真正属于「我们的」茶道开始了。十五世纪后半叶，在足利义政将军的庇护下，茶道终成体系，成为一种独立而世俗的艺术。

Since then, Teaism is fully established in Japan. Teaism, with us, became more than an idealization of the form of drinking — it is a religion of the art of life.

自此，茶道在日本完全确立。对我们而言，茶道远不止是品饮形式的理想化——它是一种生活艺术的宗教。

## 结语 · Coda · 一味同心

He who can make of himself one with the things in all their dignity, who can understand the unspoken eloquence of nature, the spotless silence of art, the limpid depth of poetry — he is qualified to receive the Cup of Humanity.

能将自身与万物之尊严合为一体者，能领会自然无声之雄辩、艺术之圣洁寂静、诗之澄澈深邃者——他便有资格领受这一只「人间之器」。

The tea is ready. The host awaits. The kettle sings.

茶已备妥。主人候之。釜上汤沸有声。

请入。
